Seonghyeon Kim is a recent graduate of the University of California, Riverside (UCR), where he obtained a BA in Psychology as a Preliminary Medical student. Before continuing his medical education (MD), he elected to join the research lab at UCI School of Medicine's Douglas Hospital to conduct clinical research in nephrology.

His research focuses on studying the factors that determine the quality of life (QOL) among patients who have undergone kidney transplant surgery, as well as examining their correlation with psychiatric disorders or symptoms.

Seonghyeon Kim has founded multiple organizations and continues to serve as a mentor or director within them. This commitment reflects his aspiration to follow in his father's footsteps. His father, a missionary, pastor, and theology professor at a graduate seminary, has dedicated his life to serving underrepresented communities.

Seonghyeon majored in psychology during his preliminary medicine year to broaden his understanding of human beings, aiming to enhance his skills as a future physician and provide compassionate care to patients.

Additionally, he founded several organizations, both affiliated with UCR and operating independently as NGOs. Some of these initiatives align with his interest in medicine, particularly psychiatry, while one was created to support first-generation students, like himself, in successfully navigating their career pathways in medicine and various healthcare fields.

Here are a few of the organizations he has created and currently lead:

  • A Daily Dose of Medicine is a nonprofit initiative he co-founded with his computer science friends—one from UCSD and the other one from Korea University.
  • OCAPUCR © was founded to offer free mentorship programs, aiming to assist pre-medical, dental, and pharmacy students in acquiring valuable information to prepare for their future careers.
  • You Deserve to be Loved was founded to deliver therapeutic messages and encouragement, contributing to the improvement of our UCR campus community.

Organization:

A Daily Dose of Medicine

This company is dedicated to provide the audience with short and quick information about some of the exciting aspects of medicine and biomedical science in general through a notification app. He led and directed a group of talented engineers through the final stages of building the app. Additionally, he plans to offer up to 70 internship positions to pre-health students who are interested in becoming our general editor interns. This initiative is designed to provide them with opportunities to gain valuable internship experiences.

OCAPUCR ©

This organization is dedicated to assist pre-medical, dental, and pharmacy students in obtaining valuable information to prepare for their future careers. He has successfully guided more than 75 students to succeed in their bachelor and post-bachelor careers, utilizing various resources. These resources include a mini book series, free tutoring sessions for classes, workshops to find shadowing positions, and conferences aimed at enhancing understanding of the matriculation process for medical schools and preparing the application.

You Deserve to be Loved

This organization is dedicated to delivering therapeutic messages and encouragement to students who request them through our Google form. They have provided support to more than 20 students, contributing to the improvement of our UCR campus community by offering an online sanctuary during their most challenging times.
